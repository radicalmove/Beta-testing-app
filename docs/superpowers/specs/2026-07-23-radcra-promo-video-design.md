# RADCRA Promo Video Design

## Purpose

Create a mostly serious, lightly parodic internal promo for FLD colleagues showing what has been built in RADCRA, the Rapid App Development Course Review App. The finished video should run about 75 seconds and never exceed 90 seconds.

The opening may evoke the controlled tension, short phrasing, and escalating competence of a familiar cinematic thriller speech, but the narration must be original and must not imitate Liam Neeson's voice or reproduce lines from *Taken*.

## Audience and outcome

The audience is people in FLD who already understand course review, Moodle, Rise, learning-design workflows, and the cost of scattered feedback. The video should leave them with three impressions:

1. RADCRA solves a recognisable review problem.
2. It already supports a credible end-to-end workflow, including role-specific views.
3. The current app is the foundation for more ambitious automation and integrations.

## Creative approach

Use a cinematic hybrid: approximately 10–12 seconds of restrained thriller-style setup, followed by a fast real-product demonstration, then a short roadmap sequence and final RADCRA lock-up.

The visual treatment is black, white, and RADCRA teal. Use Poppins, or the closest clean sans-serif available. Prefer hard cuts and brief fades through black. Avoid fake film damage, stock action imagery, conspicuous transitions, or anything that makes the internal showcase feel like a trailer template.

## Running order

| Time | Story beat | Principal visual |
|---|---|---|
| 0–12s | Controlled, parodic setup | Black, restrained text, fragmented app close-ups |
| 12–22s | RADCRA reveal | Product name and toolbar opening |
| 22–35s | Feedback in context | Highlight, pin, evidence, reply |
| 35–50s | Role-specific views | Beta Tester, Learning Designer, and SME views |
| 50–59s | Context and course navigation | Comment jump, Moodle-to-Rise recovery |
| 59–71s | Coming next | Four roadmap cards |
| 71–75s | Final lock-up | RADCRA name and closing line |

## Final narration

The recording script contains approximately 126 words. Read it slowly and deliberately, leaving the line breaks as short pauses:

> Your course has a problem.
>
> Your reviewers will find it.
>
> The harder part is finding their feedback again.
>
> After more review cycles than I care to count, I assembled an unusually specific set of skills.
>
> Then I built RADCRA: Rapid App Development Course Review App.
>
> RADCRA puts feedback where the problem lives.
>
> Highlight the words. Pin the screen. Add evidence. Start the conversation.
>
> Beta testers see their own feedback.
>
> Learning designers see the full review.
>
> Subject-matter experts see what has been shared with them.
>
> The right feedback. The right people. The right view.
>
> Across Moodle pages and Rise courses, every comment keeps its context.
>
> Resolve the issue. Move to the next. Keep the review in one place.
>
> And this is only the beginning.
>
> Virtual beta testers.
>
> Storyline integration.
>
> An API for CUBE.
>
> And more.
>
> RADCRA.
>
> Problems can't hide.

Make a rough recording before editing. If it exceeds 75 seconds, shorten pauses before speeding the delivery. If it is under 70 seconds, lengthen the opening and final pauses rather than adding words.

## Screen-capture plan

Record independent 8–12 second clips at 1920×1080 and 30 fps. Keep browser zoom at 100%, hide bookmarks and personal tabs, disable notifications, and use fictional accounts and comments. Capture one deliberate action per clip rather than attempting a continuous take.

Required clips:

1. Clean course page and pointer approaching the RADCRA toolbar.
2. Toolbar and review panel opening.
3. Selecting a short sentence and saving a highlighted-text comment.
4. Placing and saving a marker over a visual or layout issue.
5. Adding a screenshot or file to a thread.
6. Showing a reply beneath the original comment.
7. Beta Tester comment list containing only that tester's visible threads.
8. Learning Designer view of complete course feedback, status controls, and **Share with SME**.
9. SME view of a conversation shared with that SME.
10. Clicking a list comment and jumping to its exact location.
11. Navigating from Moodle into Rise and recovering the relevant marker or highlight.

Typing, uploads, waits, and long navigation may run at 2–4×. Return to normal speed immediately before the result appears.

## Roadmap cards

The closing roadmap sequence gives the detailed virtual-tester promise four seconds, followed by three concise two-second cards:

1. **Virtual beta testers** — subtitle: **Automated spelling, grammar & common-issue checks**.
2. **Storyline integration** — review support for Articulate Storyline content.
3. **CUBE API** — an API enabling integration with CUBE software.
4. **Other applications** — a deliberately open-ended suggestion that RADCRA's review model can extend further.

Cards should share one restrained visual system and remain readable without narration. Purpose-made stills or short generated clips may be produced later, after their exact art direction is approved.

## Editing workflow

Use iMovie on macOS for the quickest implementation. Build the timeline around the completed narration first, use black placeholders beneath it, then replace placeholders with captures. Add titles and subtle zooms, speed up dead time, add quiet tense music last, and export at 1080p.

Audio should remain intelligible at all times. Use one subtle impact for the RADCRA reveal and smaller accents for the roadmap cards. Review once with headphones and once muted; the muted version should still communicate the product flow through visuals and labels.

## Acceptance criteria

- Runtime is approximately 75 seconds and no longer than 90 seconds.
- The opening is recognisably playful without quoting *Taken* or impersonating Liam Neeson.
- Real product footage demonstrates contextual feedback, conversation, and navigation.
- Beta Tester, Learning Designer, and SME views are visibly distinct and accurately described.
- Moodle and Rise are both represented.
- All four roadmap themes appear clearly at the end.
- No private data, browser notifications, personal tabs, or real confidential feedback appears.
- Narration is clear over music, and the video remains understandable when muted.
